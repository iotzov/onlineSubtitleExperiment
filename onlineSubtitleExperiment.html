Noisy Auditory Perception Experiment

This is a psychology experiment to test your ability to clearly hear speech in noisy environments. In this experiment, you will be shown 3 videos that are approximately 15 minutes in length. Each of the videos will feature a speaker that is on-stage telling a story. There will be a large amount of background noise that will make hearing the speaker difficult. During the video, you will see words appear along the bottom of the screen. Whenever you hear the speaker say the word that is at the bottom of the screen, please respond by hitting the space bar on your keyboard. There will be a short demonstration video following these instructions to familiarize you with the format. Your score will be displayed below the video after you have completed it.

By continuing, you are consenting to participate in a study on auditory perception performed by researchers at the City College of New York. By participating, you are confirming that you are over 18 years of age and do not have any hearing loss. Your anonimity will be protected. Your participation in this research is completely voluntary, and you may withdraw your consent at any time. If you have any questions about the research or would like to see a report when it is completed, please reach out to Ivan Iotzov at iiotzov@ccny.cuny.edu.
